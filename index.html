STUDIO

DHYAI develops domestic objects through material-led study.

Each form begins with fibre — how it falls, carries weight, catches light, and responds to touch.

Proportion and repetition guide the structure quietly.

Forms are introduced with restraint, intended to settle naturally within lived space.

The work does not seek attention.
It seeks permanence.

Material carries memory.

MATERIAL

Material carries behaviour.

Cotton softens and settles over time.
Pigment deepens where it gathers within fibre.

Repetition, carried across scale, quietly structures the room.

As objects age, tone grows warmer and texture more familiar.

Design anticipates this change.

DOMESTIC LIFE

Objects integrate slowly into routine.

A fold introduces shadow.
Weight settles across a bed.
Light moves across surface through the day.

Use completes the form.

The interior shapes the final expression of each piece.
