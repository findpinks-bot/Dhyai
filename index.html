Studio

DHYAI develops domestic objects through material-led study.

Each form begins with fibre — how it falls, carries weight, catches light, and responds to touch.

Proportion and repetition establish structure. Surface emerges through process.

Production remains measured. Forms are introduced deliberately, with attention to how they settle within lived space.

Material

Material carries behaviour.

Density influences fall. Fibre determines breathability. Pigment settles differently across weaves.

Repetition, carried across scale, quietly structures a room.

As objects age, tone deepens and texture softens — a progression considered from the beginning of design.

Domestic Life

Objects enter routine.

A fold introduces shadow. Contact establishes wear. Placement creates rhythm within a space.

These gestures are not staged; they accumulate naturally.

The interior completes the object.

Continuity

DHYAI develops objects intended to remain.

They integrate gradually, gaining continuity through daily use.

Time completes the work.

Objects

Current studies include percale bedding, muslin dohars, bedcovers, table linen, and ritual vessels.

Each is shaped through the same material discipline and introduced gradually.
